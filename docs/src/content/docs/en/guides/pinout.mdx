<Aside type="caution">
This project was built using the ESP32 microcontroller, if you don't have experience using it we recommend you to watch the intro tutorials.

| Pin | Motor | Input | Type |
|---|---|---|---|
| GPIO 12 | Motor 1 | IN1 | PWM |
| GPIO 13 | Motor 1 | IN2 | PWM |
| GPIO 25 | Motor 1 | IN1 | DIGITAL |
| GPIO 26 | Motor 1 | IN2 | DIGITAL |
| GPIO 17 | Motor 2 | IN1 | PWM |
| GPIO 16 | Motor 2 | IN2 | PWM |
| GPIO 19 | Motor 2 | IN1 | DIGITAL |
| GPIO 18 | Motor 2 | IN2 | DIGITAL |
| GPIO 27 | Motor 1 | PWM | PWM |
| GPIO 14 | Motor 1 | ENABLE | DIGITAL |
| GPIO 23 | Motor 2 | PWM | PWM |
| GPIO 22 | Motor 2 | ENABLE | DIGITAL |

<Aside type="tip">
The **GPIO 22**/**GPIO 14** and **GPIO 14**/**GPIO 27** pins are for H-bridges that require a separate pwm input, or enable is required above 0v input voltage. Above you can see that **GPIO 14** is the enabler for motor 1 and **GPIO 22** is the enabler for motor 2. Same case with **GPIO 27** and **GPIO 23** for PWM.
</Aside>

<Aside type="tip">
If your H-bridge is based on `IN1` and `IN2` with a PWM signal, you can use the respective pins, but if you need a smoother turn, simply jumper the **GPIO 32** pin to GND.
</Aside>
